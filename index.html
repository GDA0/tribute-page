Meursault (The Stranger)

The Absurd Hero of Albert Camus's Existential Masterpiece

Here's a timeline of Meursault's life:

  • Early Life: Indifferent and detached from societal norms.
  • Mother's Death: Displays emotional detachment, focusing on physical discomfort.
  • Relationship with Marie: Driven by physical attraction rather than emotional connection.
  • The Murder: Kills an Arab man on a beach, leading to his arrest.
  • Trial and Imprisonment: Faces trial more for his character and beliefs than the actual crime.
  • Final Reflections: Accepts the absurdity of life and finds peace in his existential beliefs.
